    WHAT IS MBTI?

    The personality test known as the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI) was originally made public in 1942 by Katharine Cook Briggs and her daughter Isabel Briggs Myers.
    Carl Jung's psychology theory, as cited in The Atlantic, serves as the foundation for this concept.

    According to MBTI, a person's personality is divided into various dichotomous categories, such as
    Introversion (I) vs. Extraversion (E)
    Intuitive (N) vs. Sensory (S)
    Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F)
    Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P)
